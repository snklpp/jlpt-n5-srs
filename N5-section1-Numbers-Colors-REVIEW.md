# A. Numbers 1–10

## 一 (ichi) — "one"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *EE-chee*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** **ITCHY** — you scratch **ONE** itch. (Think *Itchy & Scratchy* from The Simpsons — there's only ever **one** Itchy.)
- **Hindi:** **इच्छा (ichchhā)** = "a wish." A genie grants you just **ONE इच्छा**.
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **一** = a single horizontal stroke. *Visual story:* one finger laid flat on the table = **1**.
**4. Quick Review:** *One ITCHY finger lies flat (一) — make your one इच्छा (wish).*

## 二 (ni) — "two"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *NEE*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** **KNEE** — you have **TWO** knees. (The *Knights Who Say "Ni!"* demand **two** shrubberies.)
- **Hindi:** **नीचे (neeche)** = "down." Bend your **TWO** knees to sit **नीचे**.
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **二** = two stacked strokes. *Visual story:* two fingers, two lines = **2**.
**4. Quick Review:** *Two KNEES bend नीचे (down) — two lines (二).*

## 三 (san) — "three"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *SAHN*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** **SUN** — three hills with a rising **SUN**; or the **SON** who is the 3rd child.
- **Hindi:** **साँप (sānp)** = "snake." **THREE साँप (snakes)** coil up into the three strokes.
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **三** = three stacked strokes. *Visual story:* three sticks laid down = **3**.
**4. Quick Review:** *Three साँप (snakes) sun-bathe under the SUN (san) — three lines (三).*

## 四 (yon / shi) — "four"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *YOHN*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** **YAWN** — you're so bored you **YAWN** four times.
- **Hindi:** **योगी (yogī)** = "yogi." A **YOGI** balances on all **FOUR** limbs.
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **四** = **囗** (enclosure / four walls) + **儿** (legs splitting inside). *Visual story:* a window with a split frame = **four** panes.
**4. Quick Review:** *A YAWN-ing YOGI (yon) sits inside four walls (四).*

## 五 (go) — "five"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *GOH*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** **"GO!"** — *5-4-3-2-1… GO!* You launch on **FIVE**.
- **Hindi:** **गोल (gol)** = "round / a goal." Five fingers curl into a **गोल (circle)**; raise five to score a **गोल**.
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **五** = two level lines (heaven & earth) with a crossing tangle between. *Visual story:* a hand with five fingers crossing over each other = **5**.
**4. Quick Review:** *"GO!" — five fingers make a गोल (circle), tangled in 五.*

## 六 (roku) — "six"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *ROH-ku*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** **"ROCK"** you — *We Will **Rock** You*, **SIX** times over.
- **Hindi:** **रोकू (rokoo)** = "I'll stop" (from *रोकना*). "I'll count and then **रोकू (stop)** at **SIX**."
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **六** = **亠** (a lid / roof) + **八** (two legs). *Visual story:* a little table standing on legs — six is "a roof you rest under after work."
**4. Quick Review:** *We will ROCK you, then रोकू (stop) at SIX (六).*

## 七 (nana) — "seven"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *NA-na*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** **NANA** (grandma) just turned **SEVEN**-ty.
- **Hindi:** **नाना (nānā)** = "maternal grandfather." Your **नाना** is **SEVEN**-ty years young.
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **七** = a horizontal stroke cut by a hooked stroke. *Visual story:* a knife "cutting" — seven is the number you reach when you slash a tally.

## 八 (hachi) — "eight"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *HA-chi*)

## 九 (kyuu) — "nine"

**4. Quick Review:** *"क्यों (why) am I NINTH in the QUEUE?" — the bent hook of 九.*

## 十 (juu / too) — "ten"

**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **十** = a plus sign / cross. *Visual story:* a crossroads where roads from **all** directions meet — "ten" = completeness, the full set.
**4. Quick Review:** *TEN TOES (too) point at तू (you) — a complete crossroads (十).*

# B. Big Numbers

## 百 (hyaku) — "hundred"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *HYA-ku*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** A war-cry **"HYAH-KOO!"** shouted by **a HUNDRED** charging samurai.
- **Hindi:** **याक (yāk)** = "yak." A herd of **a HUNDRED याक (yaks)** thunders past.
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **百** = **一** (one) on top of **白** (white). *Visual story:* **one** clean **white** sheet of paper, ruled into a **hundred** squares.
**3. Putting It Together:** one (一) + white (白) → the first/whole white count = **百 (hundred)**.
**4. Quick Review:** *A HUNDRED याक (yaks) yell "HYAH!" across one (一) white (白) field (百).*

## 千 (sen) — "thousand"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *SEN*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** **SENT** — a **THOUSAND** emails **sent** in one click.
- **Hindi:** **सेना (senā)** = "army." A **सेना (army)** a **THOUSAND** strong marches by.
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **千** = **丿** (a person striding, 𠂉) over **十** (ten). *Visual story:* a person standing tall over "ten" — ten hundreds piled up = a **thousand**.
**4. Quick Review:** *A सेना (army) of a THOUSAND, orders SENT — one person striding over ten (千).*

## 万 (man) — "ten thousand"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *MAHN*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** **MAN** — an army of **TEN-THOUSAND MEN**.
- **Hindi:** **मन (man)** = "mind / heart." **TEN-THOUSAND** thoughts swirl in your **मन (mind)**.
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **万** = a single tangled, looping figure. *Visual story:* one impossibly tangled knot — too many loops to count = "**a myriad**, ten thousand."
**4. Quick Review:** *TEN-THOUSAND MEN, TEN-THOUSAND thoughts in your मन (man) — one giant tangle (万).*

## 億 (oku) — "hundred million"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *OH-ku*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** **"OK!"** — you just won a **hundred million**: *"OK, I'm rich!"*
- **Hindi:** **ओके (OK)** — *दस करोड़ (das karoṛ)* = exactly **10 crore = a hundred million**. "**ओके!** I have **दस करोड़**."
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **億** = **亻** (person) + **意** (mind / idea). *Visual story:* a single **person (亻)** whose **mind (意)** holds a **hundred million** ideas.
**3. Putting It Together:** person (亻) + thought (意) → "as many as a person can think" = **億 (a hundred million)**.
**4. Quick Review:** *"OK / ओके — दस करोड़ (10 crore)!" A person (亻) with a hundred-million-idea mind (意) = 億.*

# C. Counting Things (the native 〜つ counter)

> All of these are **number kanji + つ** (the old Japanese counter). Focus the memory on the **sound**; the kanji is just the digit you already know.

## 一つ (hitotsu) — "one (thing)"
- **English:** **HIT a TOE** — you stub **ONE** toe. **Hindi:** **ही तो (hī to)** = "this very one!" — *"बस यही एक, **ही तो**!"*
- **Kanji:** 一 (one) + つ. **Quick:** *You HIT one TOE — ही तो (just this one) — 一つ.*

## 二つ (futatsu) — "two (things)"
- **English:** **FOOT-TATS** — **TWO** foot-tattoos. **Hindi:** **फटाफट (phaṭāphaṭ)** = "quick-quick" — grab the **TWO** *phaṭāphaṭ*.
- **Kanji:** 二 (two) + つ. **Quick:** *Two FOOT-TATS, फटाफट — 二つ.*

## 三つ (mittsu) — "three (things)"
- **English:** **MITTS** — catch with **THREE** baseball **mitts**. **Hindi:** **मिट्टी (miṭṭī)** = "soil" — **THREE** handfuls of **मिट्टी**.
- **Kanji:** 三 (three) + つ. **Quick:** *Three MITTS full of मिट्टी (soil) — 三つ.*

## 四つ (yottsu) — "four (things)"
- **English:** **YACHTS** — **FOUR yachts** in the bay. **Hindi:** **योद्धा (yoddhā)** = "warrior" — **FOUR योद्धा (warriors)**.
- **Kanji:** 四 (four) + つ. **Quick:** *Four YACHTS carry four योद्धा (warriors) — 四つ.*

## 五つ (itsutsu) — "five (things)"
- **English:** *"**IT'S TOO** much!"* — **FIVE** is too many. **Hindi:** **उत्सव (utsav)** = "festival" — **FIVE उत्सव (festivals)** in a row.
- **Kanji:** 五 (five) + つ. **Quick:** *"It's too much!" — five उत्सव (festivals) — 五つ.*

## 六つ (muttsu) — "six (things)"
- **English:** **MUTTS** — **SIX** scruffy **mutts** (dogs). **Hindi:** **मुट्ठी (muṭṭhī)** = "fist" — **SIX मुट्ठी (fistfuls)**.
- **Kanji:** 六 (six) + つ. **Quick:** *Six MUTTS, six मुट्ठी (fistfuls) of treats — 六つ.*

## 七つ (nanatsu) — "seven (things)"
- **English:** **NANA's** — grandma's **SEVEN** jars of jam. **Hindi:** **नाना (nānā)** — **नाना (grandpa)** brings **SEVEN** gifts.
- **Kanji:** 七 (seven) + つ. **Quick:** *नाना / NANA hands out SEVEN — 七つ.*

## 八つ (yattsu) — "eight (things)"
- **English:** **"YAHTZEE!"** — **EIGHT** dice in a row. **Hindi:** **यात्रा (yātrā)** = "journey" — **EIGHT यात्रा (journeys)**.
- **Kanji:** 八 (eight) + つ. **Quick:** *"Yahtzee!" — eight यात्रा (journeys) — 八つ.*

## 九つ (kokonotsu) — "nine (things)"
- **English:** **COCONUTS** — **NINE coconuts** fall. **Hindi:** **कोको + नौ (koko + nau)** — *नौ (nau)* literally **means NINE** in Hindi! "**कोको, नौ** — nine cocoas."
- **Kanji:** 九 (nine) + つ. **Quick:** *Nine COCONUTS — कोको, नौ (cocoa, nine!) — 九つ.*

## 十 (too) — "ten (things)"
- **English:** **TOE** — count **TEN toes**. **Hindi:** **तू (too)** = "you" — all **TEN** fingers point at **तू**.
- **Kanji:** 十 (ten). **Quick:** *Ten TOES point at तू (you) — 十.*

# D. How Many / How Much

## 幾つ (ikutsu) — "how many?"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *EE-ku-tsu*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** *"**EEK!** — how many are there?!"* (a shriek of counting panic).
- **Hindi:** **इकट्ठा (ikaṭṭhā)** = "gathered together." *"**इकट्ठा** कितने हैं? — how many gathered here?"*
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **幾** = **𢆶** (tiny threads) + **戍** (a guard with a halberd). *Visual story:* a guard squinting to count countless tiny threads — *"how many?!"*
**4. Quick Review:** *"EEK! इकट्ठा कितने? (how many gathered?)" — a guard counts tiny threads (幾つ).*

## 幾ら (ikura) — "how much? (price/amount)"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *EE-ku-ra*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** **IKURA** — at the sushi bar you ask, *"**Ikura** wa **ikura**?"* = "How much is the **ikura** (salmon roe)?"
- **Hindi:** **कितना (kitnā)** = "how much." *"इ-कु-रा… **कितना** हुआ? — how much did it come to?"*
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **幾** (how many — see above) + **ら**. *Visual story:* same "counting countless things," now for **price**.
**4. Quick Review:** *"Ikura wa ikura? कितना? (how much?)" — counting coins like threads (幾ら).*

# E. Number Compounds

## 一番 (ichiban) — "first, best, number one"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *EE-chee-bahn*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** **Ichiban** ramen — the brand literally means **"No. 1"**; the **best** bowl.
- **Hindi:** **बाण (bāṇ)** = "arrow." Arjuna's **first, best बाण (arrow)** — *ichi-bāṇ* hits dead centre.
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **一** (one) + **番** = **釆** (claws/sorting) over **田** (field). *Visual story:* sorting the field into a numbered line-up → "your **turn / number**." With 一 = **number one**.
**3. Putting It Together:** one (一) + turn-in-the-order (番) → **the No. 1 spot = best**.
**4. Quick Review:** *Ichiban ramen, Arjuna's first बाण (arrow) — one (一) at the head of the line-up (番).*

## 一人 (hitori) — "one person, alone"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *hee-TOH-ree*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** **HEATER** — sitting **ALONE** by the **heater**.
- **Hindi:** **ही तोड़ी (hī toṛī)** — *"अकेले **ही तोड़ी** ज़िंदगी"*… or just **"अकेला (akelā) = alone."**
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **一** (one) + **人** (person — two legs walking). *Visual story:* exactly **one** walking figure.
**3. Putting It Together:** one (一) + person (人) = **one person / by oneself**.
**4. Quick Review:** *ONE person, अकेला (alone) by the HEATER — 一 + 人 (一人).*

## 二人 (futari) — "two people"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *foo-TAH-ree*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** **FERRARI** — **TWO** people cruising in a **Ferrari**.
- **Hindi:** **फेरी (pherī)** = "a round/trip" — **TWO** friends out on a **फेरी (stroll)**.
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **二** (two) + **人** (person). *Visual story:* **two** walking figures, side by side.
**3. Putting It Together:** two (二) + person (人) = **two people**.
**4. Quick Review:** *TWO people in a फेरारी/Ferrari (futari) — 二 + 人 (二人).*

## 全部 (zenbu) — "all, the whole lot"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *ZEN-boo*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** A **ZEN BOO**k read cover to cover — **all** of it.
- **Hindi:** **जेब (jeb)** = "pocket." Empty your **whole जेब (pocket)** — **all** of it, *zen-bu*.
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **全** = **𠆢** (roof) over **王** (king/jade) = "whole, complete." **部** = **咅** + **阝** (village/section) = "a part, a division."
- *Visual story:* the **whole (全)** kingdom **plus every department (部)** = absolutely everything.
**3. Putting It Together:** whole (全) + every part (部) = **all, the entire lot**.
**4. Quick Review:** *Read the whole ZEN BOOk, empty the जेब (pocket) — whole (全) + every part (部) = 全部.*

## 半分 (hanbun) — "half"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *HAN-boon*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** **HALF a HAM-BUN** — you eat *hambun* of the bun.
- **Hindi:** **आधा (ādhā)** = "half"; sound *han-bun* ≈ **"हाँ, बन (hāṇ, bun)"** — "yes, **half** the बन (bun)."
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **半** = **八** (split) over **干** (a pole) = "cut down the middle." **分** = **八** (divide) + **刀** (knife) = "to split a portion."
- *Visual story:* split-down-the-middle (半) + cut-into-portions (分) = exactly **one half**.
**3. Putting It Together:** halved (半) + portion (分) = **a half-portion**.
**4. Quick Review:** *HALF a HAM-बन (bun), आधा (half) — split (半) into a portion (分) = 半分.*

## 沢山 (takusan) — "a lot, many"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *TAK-sahn*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** **TUCK a TON** away — you stash **a lot**, *son!*
- **Hindi:** **ठाकुर साहब (Ṭhākur sāhab)** owns **a lot** of land — *tāku-san* ≈ **Thakur-san**.
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **沢** = **氵** (water) + **尺** (measure) = "a marsh, lots of water." **山** = "mountain."
- *Visual story:* a whole **marsh (沢)** of water **plus a mountain (山)** piled high = **a huge amount**.
**3. Putting It Together:** swamp-ful (沢) + mountain (山) = **tons, a lot**.
**4. Quick Review:** *TUCK a TON, ठाकुर साहब's wealth — a marsh (沢) and a mountain (山) = 沢山.*

## 番号 (bangou) — "a number (in a series)"
**1. Sound Trick** (Pronunciation: *BAN-goh*)
- **English / Pop-culture:** **"BINGO!"** — your **number** is called. (*Ban-go!*)
- **Hindi:** **बंगला (banglā)** = "bungalow." The **number** painted on the **बंगला (bungalow)** gate.
**2. Kanji Breakdown**
- **番** = **釆** (sorting) + **田** (field) = "turn / order in a line." **号** = **口** (mouth) + **丂** (a shout) = "a call sign, a number."
- *Visual story:* your **place in line (番)** announced by a **shout (号)** = your **number**.
**3. Putting It Together:** turn-in-order (番) + the called sign (号) = **a number in a series**.
**4. Quick Review:** *"BINGO!" the बंगला (bungalow) number is called — order (番) + shout-out (号) = 番号.*
